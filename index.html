Privacy Policy

Capora — Personal Finance App · Last updated: April 5, 2026

In short: Capora does not sell your data. By default, your finance data stays on your device. Cloud Sync is OFF by default and is only used if you explicitly enable it in Settings. Cloud Sync requires a Google account. If you enable Cloud Sync, your data is processed and stored by Google Firebase so synchronization can work across your devices.

1. Who We Are

Capora is a personal finance application developed as an independent project. For the purposes of this policy, "we", "us", and "our" refer to the developer of Capora. For any privacy-related inquiries, contact us at cshflw2026@gmail.com.

This policy applies to the Capora Android application and any associated web-based interfaces.

2. What Data We Collect

Data you enter into the app

Capora stores only the financial data you manually input:

  • Transactions (amount, description, category, date, type)
  • Recurring expenses and income (templates, frequency, amounts)
  • Savings goals and jar contributions
  • Budget limits per category
  • App settings (starting balance, preferred currency, language, notification preferences)
  • Financial scenarios you create

Capora does not use analytics or advertising trackers. We do not collect location data, contacts, call history, SMS content, camera, microphone, or similar device sensors. We do not collect device identifiers (IMEI, advertising ID).

Data collected when Cloud Sync is enabled

If you enable Cloud Sync, the following is additionally processed by Firebase:

  • Your Google account email address and Firebase user ID (used solely to identify your sync account)
  • All app data listed above, uploaded to your private Firestore document
  • Standard server-side technical logs maintained by Google (IP address, request timestamps, Firebase SDK version) — these are managed by Google under its own privacy policy and are not accessible to us
  • A last-sync timestamp stored in Firestore

Household / Shared data (only if you use it)

The Household feature allows you to share your financial data with another person (e.g. a partner or family member). If you use this feature:

  • All household members can read and write the shared financial data
  • Household data is stored in a separate Firestore document accessible to all members you invite
  • Only invite people you trust — shared data includes your full transaction history, templates, budgets, and savings
  • You can leave a household at any time; the app will fork a private copy of the data for your own use

Notifications

If you enable reminders, Capora requests the Android POST_NOTIFICATIONS permission. Notification scheduling is performed locally on your device using the Capacitor Local Notifications plugin. Notification content is never transmitted to any server.

3. Where Data Is Stored

Local storage (always, regardless of Cloud Sync)

Your data is primarily stored locally on your Android device using the WebView's local storage (key prefix cf4_). This data does not leave your device unless you explicitly enable Cloud Sync.

Note about device backups: Depending on your Android and Google account backup settings, app data may be included in Android system backups managed by your device or Google. This is controlled by your OS settings, not by Capora.

Cloud Sync (optional, Google account required)

Cloud Sync is disabled by default. When you explicitly enable it in Settings, you will be prompted to sign in with a Google account. Once enabled, your data is stored in Google Firebase:

  • Firebase Firestore — stores your financial data in a private document at users/{your-firebase-uid}/cashflow/data, or in a household document at households/{householdId} if you use the Household feature
  • Firebase Authentication — stores your Google sign-in session to authenticate data access

Firebase data is hosted on Google Cloud infrastructure. Data may be stored in data centers outside your country of residence, including the United States. Google LLC is certified under the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework, providing an adequate level of protection for transfers from the European Economic Area.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), our legal bases for processing your data are:

  • Performance of a contract / provision of service (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): Processing of locally stored data is necessary to provide app functionality (calculating balances, displaying transactions, etc.).
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR): Cloud Sync and Google Sign-In are entirely optional. By enabling Cloud Sync and signing in with your Google account, you give explicit consent for your data to be uploaded to Firebase. You may withdraw this consent at any time by disabling Cloud Sync in Settings.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): Technical logging by Google Firebase (IP addresses, request logs) may be processed on the basis of legitimate interests in providing reliable infrastructure. These logs are governed by Google's own privacy policy.

5. Third Parties

Capora does not sell your personal data and does not use advertising networks.

Service providers used only when you enable the relevant feature:

  • Google Firebase (Firestore + Authentication) — for optional Cloud Sync. Governed by Google Firebase Privacy Policy.
  • Google Sign-In — used to authenticate your identity for Cloud Sync. Governed by Google Privacy Policy.

We do not use:

  • Crash reporting (including Crashlytics / Sentry)
  • Advertising SDKs or ad networks
  • Social media SDKs (Facebook, Twitter/X, TikTok, etc.)
  • Data brokers or data enrichment services

6. Data Security

  • Local data is protected by Android operating system sandboxing mechanisms.
  • Cloud data is protected by Firebase Security Rules that restrict Firestore document access to the authenticated owner's Firebase UID, and household documents to verified household members only.
  • All communication with Firebase services uses HTTPS/TLS encryption.
  • Capora does not store passwords, card numbers, IBANs, account numbers, PINs (raw), or similar sensitive banking credentials.
  • The in-app PIN lock stores only a verification value (hash) locally — the raw PIN is never stored or transmitted.

Despite these measures, no system is 100% secure. We recommend using a strong Google account password and enabling 2-factor authentication on your Google account.

7. Data Retention

Local data

Local data is retained on your device until you delete it via the app's Reset function, or until you uninstall the app. Uninstalling the app removes all local data.

Cloud data (if Cloud Sync was enabled)

Cloud data in Firebase Firestore is retained as long as your account is active or until you request deletion. Disabling Cloud Sync in the app stops future synchronization but does not automatically delete data already stored in Firebase.

To permanently delete your cloud data, contact us at cshflw2026@gmail.com with the email address of the Google account you used. We will process deletion requests within 30 days.

Firebase Authentication session data

Firebase Authentication session tokens are managed by Google and expire according to Google's standard token lifecycle. Signing out of Google account in the app terminates the active session.

8. Your Rights and Controls

Depending on your jurisdiction (including under GDPR for EEA residents), you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: You can view all your data inside the app at any time.
  • Right to data portability: You can export your data (JSON format) using the Export feature in the app drawer, if available in your build.
  • Right to rectification: You can edit or correct any transaction, template, or setting directly in the app.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): You can delete local data via the Reset function in Settings. For cloud data deletion, contact us at cshflw2026@gmail.com.
  • Right to withdraw consent: You can disable Cloud Sync at any time in Settings > Cloud Sync > Disconnect Google Account. This stops all future synchronization.
  • Right to object / restrict processing: Contact us at cshflw2026@gmail.com to exercise these rights.

If you believe your rights have not been respected, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.
